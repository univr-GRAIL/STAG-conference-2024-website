Information

STAG 2024 conference will take place at the headquarters of the Order of Engineers of Verona and Province (Ordine degli Ingegneri di Verona e Provincia), located in Verona, Italy.
Address: Via Santa Teresa, 12, 37131 Verona VR, Italy.




Welcome to Verona!

Verona in northern Italy is a city known for its romantic atmosphere and historical charm. With its well-preserved medieval old town along the Adige River, Verona offers visitors a glimpse into the past with its cobblestone streets, ancient churches, and hidden piazzas

Famous for Shakespeare's Romeo and Juliet, Verona attracts tourists to Juliet's House, where they can see the iconic balcony and leave love notes. The city's Verona Arena, a Roman amphitheater, hosts opera performances and concerts, showcasing its rich cultural heritage

Verona welcomes visitors with its warm and friendly locals, adding to the city's inviting atmosphere. From exploring historical sites to enjoying local cuisine, Verona offers a blend of experiences for travelers seeking Italian culture and romance.

How to get to Verona

If you get to Verona internationally, the suggested route is to fly to the Verona Valerio Catullo airport and then take the shuttle bus to the Verona Porta Nuova train station.
The train station is both close to the city center and the venue, and well connected by buses.
From the train station, you can reach the venue by bus.

By plane: the Valerio Catullo airport is about 8 km from Verona. A shuttle bus service (line 199) runs from the airport to the Verona Porta Nuova train station train station every 20 minutes. Ticket is 7€, is valid for 75 minutes and while valid it can also be used for the urban bus routes in Verona. You can also buy your ticket online or using the "Ticket Bus Verona" app. Visit the local transpotation (ATV) website for more information here.
From the train station you can take several buses to reach the venue (see below).

By train: the Verona Porta Nuova train station is about 3 km from the venue. It is well connected for national travellers and for German/Austrian visitors on the DB and ÖBB Raiilways.
From there you can take several buses to reach the venue (see below).

By bus: the venue is well connected by bus. From the train station you can choose between several lines:

  • Lines 21, 51, 52: from "Stazione Porta Nuova E2" (sidewalk E2) to bus stop "Via Santa Teresa" (2 stops)
  • Line 41: from "Stazione Porta Nuova E2" (sidewalk E2) to bus stop "Via Tombetta III B" (2 stops)

There are also other bus lines that can be used to reach the venue, but are less convenient (lines 73, 23, 24, 22, 25).
For more information, visit the local transportation (ATV) website here.
